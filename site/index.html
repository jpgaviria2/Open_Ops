The merchant reality

Small merchants are running modern businesses on a pile of disconnected tools.

Independent operators need the same digital leverage as big chains, but their tools rarely talk to each other.

  • POS data lives in one dashboard.
  • Ordering and loyalty live somewhere else.
  • Staff checklists are paper, spreadsheets, or Notion pages.
  • Inventory, waste, and discrepancies are reported manually.
  • Customer communication is scattered across social, SMS, DMs, and email.
  • AI tools exist, but they do not understand the actual business.

The pain is not lack of software. It is too much isolated software, which creates owner fatigue and operational blind spots.

Rewards API

BTCPay-backed Bitcoin rewards plugin powers wallet balance, history, claims, redemptions, and merchant-defined sats loyalty.

Checkout rails

Lightning invoices can settle customer orders while the operational order still lands in Square/KDS as paid.

Merchant control

The merchant owns the server, store, Lightning node, reward policy, and settlement path — no closed points database required.

Trails reference stack: BTCPay Server at btcpay.anmore.me, Bitcoin Rewards API, Lightning invoice flows, CAD↔sats conversion, and Square external-payment compatibility for kitchen routing.

Explain this as practical Bitcoin: rewards that are real value, invoices that can settle orders, and a merchant-controlled system rather than a speculative crypto feature.
